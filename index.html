Earthquake Safety

This online checklist is created to help you plan and brave a major earthquake in your area. By taking the right measures, the effect of an earthquake can be reduced. Shifting off the ground is rarely the real reason for death or injury. Most deaths result from collapsing buildings, hurling projectiles, and falling debris. However, it is always important to develop your earthquake preparedness because earthquakes can occur with no sign. Gather up some emergency supplies and get ready to become earthquake prepared!

  • Earthquake Safety Checklist

    • Have on Hand For any Emergency

    1. Emergency Contact Information - Jot down three along with their respective addresses and an out-of-town relative or friend as a point-of-contact for separated family members.
    2. Flashlight - Always have one at your disposal along with extra batteries. Flashlights are better to have on hand rather than candles, in order to prevent any unnecessary fires if an unknown gas leak occurs due to the earthquake.
    3. Portable Radio - Similar to the Flashlight, a Portable Radio is good to have on hand because in the event of an earthquake, there will be outages and it will become your best source to find out what is going on and what to do next.
    4. First Aid Kit - This and knowledge of first aid and CPR will come in handy if you or anyone around you has been injured or has gone into cardiac arrest. (include link of Red Cross courses)
    5. Fire Extinguisher - In case of any small fires in or around your area, make sure you know how to use one and what types of fires they may put out. Your local fire department can train you!
    6. Food and Water - Having non-perishable food and plenty of water on hand will come in handy in case of a power outage or any other circumstance. You should consider buying enough food that can last for at least 72 hours. Stock up on canned or dehydrated foods, dried fruits, canned juices, and powdered milk. To stock up on water, make sure you have three gallons per person for a 72-hour period. You can clean your water with purification tablets like Halazone and Globaline.
    7. Medications and Special Diets - If you live with any pre-existing medical condition in which you need special medication, make sure to have a week’s worth of supply. For those families with infants, be prepared to stock up on special foods.
    8. Tools - Check if you have wrenches to turn off any gas and water mains.

    Home Preparedness

    Educate your household how to turn off electricity, gas, and water main switches and valves. However, it is crucial to leave the gas on except there is an emergency.

    Earthquake Hazard Hunt

    Be aware of your surroundings, it is best to note potential hazards before the earthquake strikes in order to prevent. For example, if there are any hanging or sharp objects that can potentially fall and injure someone, relocate them to a safer place.

    How to Ride out the Earthquake

    Earthquakes in general are unperceived and sudden, so there could be multiple scenarios in which you experience an earthquake.

    If you are…

    Indoors
    • Get under a desk or table or stand in a doorway or corner. It is important to remain far from any windows, tall furniture, and fireplaces.
    Outdoors
    • Find an open space where you are distant from any powerlines, buildings, or trees.
    In a Building
    • Stay on your same floor and get under a desk far from walls and windows. Do not use the elevator as it could lose power or stability.
    In a Vehicle
    • Drive to the side of the road clear of powerlines and overpasses and stop the car. Remain inside until the earthquake stops and do not drive over damaged roads or bridges.

    When the Ground Stops Shaking

    Check for Injuries
    • Verify that everyone can breath normally and if not, apply mouth-to-mouth resuscitation.
    • Check to see that everyone is wearing shoes to prevent any cuts.
    • Stop any bleeding by applying pressure to the wound. Only use the telephone if someone is seriously injured and do not move them. Cover them with a blanket to stabilize any temperature drops.
    Check for Hazards
    • Put out any fires, however if it becomes uncontrollable, leave your house and warn your neighbors.
    • Make sure any gas, water, and electrical lines or appliances are safe.
    • If there is harm to your electrical wiring, switch off electrical power.
    • Do not come into contact with damaged electrical appliances.
    • Clean up any flammable liquids.
    • Check food and water supplies.
    • Check building structure.
    • Inspect the inside of your cupboards cautiously.
    • Do not drive and try to keep streets clear unless there is an emergency.
    • Expect aftershocks.
    • Stay informed by using a battery-powered radio.

    If You Have to Evacuate

    • Leave a detailed message of where you can be found.
    • List meeting points in case of any separation. Meeting points can be schools or community centers.
    • Take medicines, first aid kits, flashlights, radios and batteries, important documents, cash, food, sleeping bags/blankets, and some extra clothes.
